# Session Summary: Thirst for Water and Secrets of Ergo

**PCs present:** James Hondo, Goran Dragic, Pika, Jayne Cobb, Fermin, Den, Lyra, Daijiro. (Note: Interactions identify players taking on the roles of their respective characters or acting as a group).
**NPCs present and mentioned:** O'Leary, Ealing, Captain Mari Jagad (mentioned), Admiral Sal Dancet (mentioned), Miria Silverhand (mentioned), Krrsh (mentioned), Polona, Goizéder (mentioned as Joyce Edwards), Pilot informant, Local beggars, Local gunners.

**Main developments in the campaign and the reconstruction of the Kingdom of Drinax:**
The group has decided to temporarily pause their direct incursion into Ergo to focus on a local problem in Hilfer: a severe water crisis caused by the piracy of Miria Silverhand. The main plan now is to hunt down this pirate to seize her ship (which possesses heavy equipment like cranes) and use the victory to gain political influence, presenting themselves as benefactors of the Kingdom of Drinax to improve the kingdom's abysmal reputation among the locals.

**Loose ends to be resolved in the future:**
*   **The mystery of the freighter *Garland*:** They discovered that this ship imported food to a radioactive and blockaded planet (Ergo), although the records were altered to say it came from Falcon. Its current whereabouts, after 5 or 6 years of being missing, are unknown.
*   **The blockade of Ergo:** An informant revealed that ships going to Ergo are intercepted at the gas giant by system ships (without jump drives) that pick up the passengers or cargo.
*   **The water conspiracy:** James Hondo suspects that Miria Silverhand is not acting alone and that there could be a corporation or a third party paying her to destabilize the planet Hilfer by cutting off its ice supplies.

**Session Joke:**
The best joke of the session arose during the attempt to hack the spaceport servers. Fermin (Nacho) was going to perform the computer roll and Den/Pika (Emilio) tried to help him using an expensive level 12 decoder. However, Emilio failed his roll miserably (rolling a 3), which led the GM and the players to joke that, instead of helping, he was "un-helping," comparing his high-tech equipment to a "ChatGPT that does things wrong." In the end, Fermin was almost caught because of this "help." Furthermore, the constant mentions of how terrible everyone in the crew is at gunnery generated quite a few laughs.

### Scene 1: Playing Cards with Ghosts of the Past
**PCs present:** Lyra (acting under the cover of a corporate prospector)
**NPCs present:** 
*   O'Leary (Face paralyzed on the right side, drools occasionally, right arm immobile, wears a crewman uniform without insignia; *Archetype: Traumatized survivor*)
*   Ealing (Drunk as a skunk; *Archetype: Veteran drowning his sorrows*)

**Location(s):** Traditional adobe bar (Hilfer)
**Ships mentioned:** 
*   *Martin II* (Unknown model, Unknown Captain - Ship assaulted by pirates)
*   *El Comino* (Unknown model, Captain Mari Jagad)
*   *Águila de Nón* / *Banshee* (Unknown model, Unknown Captain)

**Summary:**
The undercover character spends time in an adobe bar in Hilfer playing a card and dice game with the locals. There, they strike up a conversation with O'Leary and Ealing, former crew members of the ship *Martin II*. O'Leary recounts how ruthless pirates assaulted their ship, there were deaths, and then they froze the survivors, abandoning them in the desert. Because the cooler systems failed, O'Leary suffered brain damage and paralysis. They also reveal that the Empire is furious and has sent Admiral Sal Dancet to hunt pirates in the sector, and that their former commander, Mari Jagad, now captains *El Comino*, patrolling from the world of Goertel in search of an infamous pirate ship (the *Águila de Nón*).

### Scene 2: Bureaucracy and Sloppy Hacking
**PCs present:** James Hondo, Fermin, Den, Pika
**NPCs present:** None
**Location(s):** Spaceport Archives (Hilfer)
**Ships mentioned:** 
*   *Garland* (Large freighter, Unknown Captain)

**Summary:**
While James Hondo uses his social and bureaucratic skills to request legal access to old spaceport records, Fermin attempts to hack the computer systems, supported by the decryption equipment from Den/Pika. Despite poor coordination that almost tripped the alarms, they manage to extract a vital piece of data: about 13 years ago, coinciding with Drinax's conquest of Asim, a freighter named *Garland* stopped importing food from Asim and registered a strange cargo of hundreds of tons of food coming from the radioactive planet Ergo (a record that was quickly altered to say "Falcon"). The ship hasn't been seen in Hilfer for about 5 or 6 years.

### Scene 3: Paid Information and the Water Crisis
**PCs present:** James Hondo, Jayne Cobb, Daijiro
**NPCs present:** 
*   Pilot informant (Carefree, eager to get paid; *Archetype: Information mercenary*)
*   Pair of beggars (Cracked and bleeding lips, dehydrated, wearing tunics like Bedouins, the woman just lost a baby due to the conditions; *Archetype: Collateral victims*)

**Location(s):** Streets outside the bars (Hilfer)
**Ships mentioned:** None

**Summary:**
The group meets with a pilot who promises information about Ergo in exchange for 500 credits. The pilot tells them the rumor that a Tech-World engineer hired a trip to Ergo, but upon arriving at the system's gas giant, a local ship without a jump drive appeared to pick up the engineer and his cargo, proving there is a clandestine operation in orbit. After paying him, Daijiro stealthily follows the pilot into the street, but runs into a bouncer kicking out a pair of local beggars. Daijiro intercepts them and gives them a couple of credits; the beggars, with symptoms of extreme dehydration, tell him that the price of water has skyrocketed and there are no supplies, revealing the humanitarian impact of the pirate attacks on the ice harvesters.

### Scene 4: Preparations for the Hunt
**PCs present:** James Hondo, Goran Dragic, Pika, Jayne Cobb, Fermin, Den, Lyra, Daijiro
**NPCs present:** 
*   Two local gunners (Distrustful, professional, demand formal contracts; *Archetype: Swords for hire*)
*   Polona (Pilot, *Archetype: Loyal crew*)

**Location(s):** Streets, Spaceport, and Starship (Hilfer)
**Ships mentioned:** 
*   *Maui* (Unknown model, Unknown Captain)
*   *Ginger* (Unknown model, Unknown Captain)
*   *Merciful Fusion* / *MercyFuge* (400-ton corsair ship, Captain Miria Silverhand)

**Summary:**
Gathered on the *Maui*, the group debates their next move. After using communications and Den's Astrogation skill, they manage to narrow down the areas of the attacks in the asteroid belts of the system's gas giant. James Hondo proposes hunting the pirate Miria Silverhand (whose 400-ton ship is heavily armed with lasers, missiles, and sandcasters) to keep her ship, her loot, and return the flow of water to Hilfer, thus winning the political favor of the local tribes for Drinax. Realizing that their gunnery skills are pathetic, they go to the spaceport using false identities to hire two local gunners for 1,000 credits a week plus a share of the loot. With the contract signed, the crew prepares to take both ships (the *Maui* and the hidden *Ginger*) to the asteroid zone to set an ambush or comb the area in search of the pirate.
