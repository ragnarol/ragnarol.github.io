**PCs present:** Pulgar, Ceres, Erdan, Broseph.
**NPCs present:** Donavich (Tired and broken voice praying - *Archetype: Desperate Priest*), Doru (Banging wood from the sub-basement, shouting distressed and hungry - *Archetype: Hidden Monster*).

### Scene 3: Father Donavich's Secret
**PCs present:** Pulgar, Ceres, Erdan, Broseph.
**NPCs present:** Father Donavich (Man with dark circles under his eyes, dirty, disheveled, mentally broken, insane but harmless gaze - *Archetype: Tragic Father / Blind Fanatic*).
**Location(s):** Chapel and church rooms (Barovia).
**Loot obtained:** A book titled *"The Sword of Truth, The Uses of Logic in War Against Diabolical Energies as Applied by the Inquisition of Ulmiz"* (Looted by Pulgar).
**Scene Summary:**
Ceres picks the lock and uses *Thaumaturgy* to enter illuminated by an angelic halo, trying to comfort the priest. Erdan casts *Detect Thoughts* and perceives the man's madness and sadness. Donavich confesses that a year ago, a man in black led the village youths (including his 20-year-old son Doru) against Strahd's castle. They all died, but Doru returned as a monster that feeds on blood. Ceres tells him bluntly that his son is undead and that the only real help is to end his suffering. Terrified that his son will be killed, Donavich locks the basement trapdoor and refuses to feed or kill him, preferring to wait for a miracle from Lathander. Ceres manages to persuade him not to feed Doru while they seek another solution. As they discuss, Pulgar sneaks into the priest's office and steals a book about inquisitions and imps.

### Scene 4: Mari's Cry
**PCs present:** Renoir.
**NPCs present:** Mari (Distraught woman, clutching a creepy doll - *Archetype: Enraged Mother / Tragic Villager*).
**Location(s):** Mari's House (Barovia).
**Loot obtained:** None material (Information: "Blinsky" brand dolls).
**Scene Summary:**
Renoir, walking through the village, hears crying coming from a house. Finding the door locked, he forces his way in by climbing through a window to the second floor. There he finds Mari, a woman crying on the bed clutching a monstrous doll. Mari confesses that her daughter Gertruda, whom she had kept hidden from the village for 17 years to protect her, packed a bundle and fled four nights ago, stealing her key. The woman is convinced her daughter is dead or in Strahd's hands. Renoir investigates the room, confirming the young woman's premeditated escape. He also examines the doll, discovering a tag that reads: *"If it isn't fun, it isn't a Blinsky"*, a famous local toy maker. Mari refuses to give him the doll as a clue, and Renoir leaves, taking notes of everything.

### Scene 5: The Burial and Departure
**PCs present:** Pulgar, Ceres, Erdan, Renoir, Broseph.
**NPCs present:** Ismark, Ireena, Father Donavich.
**Location(s):** Barovia Cemetery.
**Loot obtained:** Silvered short sword (Given by Ireena to Renoir).
**Scene Summary:**
The group reunites and carries the Burgomaster's coffin to the cemetery, accompanied by a few apathetic villagers. A somber ceremony is officiated where Ireena mourns her father's loss. Donavich, amazed by the presence of a dwarf and an elf (races he has only seen as ghosts marching towards Ravenloft), advises them on their journey: he recommends they not stop in the city of Balaquia (Vallaki) and continue west to a settlement in Brescia called Santa Barovia (Krezk), where there will be a strong church that will protect Ireena. With their destination set, the group executes their plan: Ireena, disguised, gives her silvered short sword to Renoir after he promises to take care of her. The group prepares to leave Barovia's mist and begin their journey through the domain's dangerous roads.
